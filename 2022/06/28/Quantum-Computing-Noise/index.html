The Germanium Quantum Information

Intro

Ge has high hole mobilities, can be an ultraclean material platform for well-controlled, high-quality quantum dots (QDs).

  1. Ge/Si core/shell nanowires (NWs)
  2. Ge hut wires (HWs)

Josephson Field-Effect

Holes in Germanium

  • Magnetically and electrically driven single-spin rotations and sub-nanosecond SWAP\sqrt{\text{SWAP}}SWAP​ gates between two spins were demonstrated with conduction-band electrons in lateral Ga-As QDs.
  • Not only filled conduction-band states but also holes (unfilled valence-band states) are promising candidates for the implementation of spin qubits.
  • The potential of holes for quantum computation was underlined by the encouraging hole-spin lifetimes measured in self-assembled In-Ga-As QDs.

Heavy-hole and Light-hole States

BULK

In the electronic band structure of bulk Ge,

  1. four degenerate valence-band states of highest energy are located at the Γ\GammaΓ point.
  2. Crystal momentum ℏk\hbar kℏk of the electrons is ZERO.

By combining the degrees of freedom due to the electron spin and those due to the p-type atomic orbitals, a sixfold instead of fourfold degeneracy would be expected.

Sixfold degeneracy is lifted by a spin-orbit interaction at the atomic level.

The interaction separates the four states in which the bare spin and the atomic orbital angular momentum are parallel (effective spin 2/32/32/3) from the remaining two states, in which they are antiparallel (effective spin 1/21/21/2).

δSOl⃗⋅s⃗\delta_{SO}\vec{l}\cdot\vec{s}δSO​l⋅s

This term of type can be derived from the Dirac equation. δSO\delta_{SO}δSO​ is a coefficient, ℏl⃗\hbar\vec{l}ℏl is the angular momentum associated with the p-type atomic orbitals and ℏs⃗\hbar\vec{s}ℏs is the bare electron spin.

Below is the Quantum -Mechanical Addition of Angular Momenta

  • l⃗⋅l⃗=l(l+1)\vec{l}\cdot\vec{l}=l(l+1)l⋅l=l(l+1)
  • s⃗⋅s⃗=s(s+1)\vec{s}\cdot\vec{s}=s(s+1)s⋅s=s(s+1)

Thus, δSOl⃗⋅s⃗\delta_{SO}\vec{l}\cdot\vec{s}δSO​l⋅s has four degenerate eigenstates with j=3/2j=3/2j=3/2 and eigenenergy eigenenergy δSO/2\delta_{SO}/2δSO​/2, and two degenerate eigenstates with j=1/2j=1/2j=1/2 and eigenenergy −δSO-\delta_{SO}−δSO​.

The introduced quantum number jjj is related to the size of the effective spin via j⃗⋅j⃗=j(j+1)\vec{j}\cdot\vec{j}=j(j+1)j​⋅j​=j(j+1). The energy difference Δ0=3δSO/2\Delta_{0}=3\delta_{SO}/2Δ0​=3δSO​/2 is commonly referred to as the spin-orbit gap and separates the topmost valence band (j=3/2j=3/2j=3/2) from the spin-orbit split-off band (j=1/2j=1/2j=1/2) at the Γ\GammaΓ point

Eigenstates

The eigenstates of HLKH_{LK}HLK​ can be grouped:

  1. Heavy-hole (HH)

    The effective spin is parallel or antiparallel (spin projections of ±3ℏ/2\pm3\hbar/2±3ℏ/2) to the hole’s direction of motion (given by k⃗\vec{k}k) and the associated eigenenergy is −ℏ2k2/2mHH-\hbar^{2}k^{2}/2m_{HH}−ℏ2k2/2mHH​,where mHH=m0/(γ1−2γs)m_{HH}=m_{0}/(\gamma_{1}-2\gamma_{s})mHH​=m0​/(γ1​−2γs​) is the effective HH mass.

  2. Light-hole (LH)

    The spin projection along the direction of motion is ±ℏ/2\pm\hbar/2±ℏ/2 and the eigenenergy is −ℏ2k2/2mLH-\hbar^{2}k^{2}/2m_{LH}−ℏ2k2/2mLH​, where mLH=mo/(γ1+2γs)m_{LH}=m_{o}/(\gamma_{1}+2\gamma_{s})mLH​=mo​/(γ1​+2γs​) is the effective LH mass.
