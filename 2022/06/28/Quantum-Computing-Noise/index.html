The Germanium Quantum Information

Introduction

Ge has high hole mobilities, can be an ultraclean material platform for well-controlled, high-quality quantum dots (QDs).

  1. Ge/Si core/shell nanowires (NWs)
  2. Ge hut wires (HWs)

Holes in Germanium

  • Magnetically and electrically driven single-spin rotations and sub-nanosecond SWAP\sqrt{\text{SWAP}}SWAP​ gates between two spins were demonstrated with conduction-band electrons in lateral Ga-As QDs.
  • Not only filled conduction-band states but also holes (unfilled valence-band states) are promising candidates for the implementation of spin qubits.
  • The potential of holes for quantum computation was underlined by the encouraging hole-spin lifetimes measured in self-assembled In-Ga-As QDs.

Heavy-hole and Light-hole States

BULK

In the electronic band structure of bulk Ge,

  1. four degenerate valence-band states of highest energy are located at the Γ\GammaΓ point.
  2. Crystal momentum ℏk\hbar kℏk of the electrons is ZERO.

By combining the degrees of freedom due to the electron spin and those due to the p-type atomic orbitals, a sixfold instead of fourfold degeneracy would be expected.

Sixfold degeneracy is lifted by a spin-orbit interaction at the atomic level.

The interaction separates the four states in which the bare spin and the atomic orbital angular momentum are parallel (effective spin 2/32/32/3) from the remaining two states, in which they are antiparallel (effective spin 1/21/21/2).

PLANAR HETEROSTRUCTURES

In Ge/SiGe planar heterostructures, holes are confined to the strained Ge layer because of the valence-band offsets at the interfaces. Because the hole confinement in the out-of-plane direction zzz is much stronger than in the xxx-yyy plane, these heterostructures can be considered as quasi-2D system.

The variance of a hole’s z-coordinate is small, Heisenberg’s uncertainty principle implies that the variance of the out-of-plane momentum has a larger lower bound. Basis states that originate from the topmost valence band are usually classified according to the out-of-plane component ℏJz\hbar J_zℏJz​ of the effective spin. Which means, basis states satisfying Jz=±3/2J _ {z}=\pm3/2Jz​=±3/2 and Jz=±1/2J _ {z}=\pm1/2Jz​=±1/2 in a quasi-2D structure are commonly referred to as HH and LH states.

Removing valence-band electrons from quasi-2D Ge system usually results in hole states whose spin parts consist predominantly of states

  1. for which Jz=±3/2{J _ {z}=\pm3/2}Jz​=±3/2 (pure HHs), the kx,y{k _ {x,y}}kx,y​-dependent terms of Eq.1 simplify to −ℏ2(kx2+ky2)/2m∣∣HH{-\hbar^{2}(k _ {x}^{2}+k _ {y}^{2})/2m _ {||}^{HH}}−ℏ2(kx2​+ky2​)/2m∣∣HH​, where m∣∣HH=m0/(γ1+γs)≈0.005m0{m _ {||}^{HH}=m _ {0}/(\gamma_{1}+\gamma_s)\approx0.005m_0}m∣∣HH​=m0​/(γ1​+γs​)≈0.005m0​ is remarkably light.
  2. If, instead, Jz=±1/2{J _ {z}=\pm1/2}Jz​=±1/2 (pure LHs), m∣∣LH=m0/(γ1−γs){m _ {||}^{LH}=m _ {0}/(\gamma_{1}-\gamma_{s})}m∣∣LH​=m0​/(γ1​−γs​), meaning that HH and LH sub-bands have reversed characters for the in-plane motion.

HUT WIRES and NANOWIRES

Hut Wires

Germanium HWs grown on Si have a triangular cross section and are fully compressively strained along the in-plane directions xxx and yyy, where they adopt the Si lattice constant.

Calculations suggest that low-energy holes in Ge HWs are nearly pure HHs with a LH admixture below 111%.

Nanowires

In Ge/Si core-shell NWs, the larger valence-band offset about 500 meV at the Ge/Si interface confines holes to the Ge core.

Spin-orbit Interaction and g-factors

The diamond cubic structure has a center of inversion, which means that Ge is bulk-inversion-symmetric.

Electric-Dipole-Induced Spin Resonance in Quantum Dots

Introduction

  • ESR: Electron-spin-resonance

    With the help of only a static magnetic field and an electron-spin-resonance pulse, one can change the spin qubit at will.

  • Landeˊ g\text{Land\'{e} }gLandeˊ g Tensor

    A ggg-factor modulation resonance (ggg-TMR) occurs similarly to an ESR, when the frequency of the electric field matches the Zeeman splitting.

  • EDSR: Electric-dipole-induced Spin Resonance

    Rashba and Efros have shown that the EDSR is highly efficient in quantum wells, promising electron spin control on a time scale ωR−1≃100\omega _ {R}^{-1}\simeq100ωR−1​≃100 ps, where ωR\omega _ RωR​ is the Rabi frequency.

In quantum dots, the spin-orbit interaction is generally suppressed due to the complete localization of the electron motion. Typically, the quantum dot lateral size λd\lambda _ dλd​ is smaller than the spin-orbit length λSO\lambda _ {SO}λSO​, and any effect of the spin-orbit interaction is suppressed as a power of λd/λSO\lambda _ d/\lambda _ {SO}λd​/λSO​ and therefore is expected to be weak. (λSO\lambda _ {SO}λSO​越小，自旋轨道耦合越强，which means the spin-orbit coupling is stronger when λSO\lambda _ {SO}λSO​ is smaller)

In this paper, we consider the use of EDSR for control of individual electron spins in quantum dots.

There are two major mechanisms of EDSR in quantum dots.

  1. Arises from the linear in momentum Dresselhaus and Rashba spin-orbit couplings in combination with Zeeman interaction.
  2. The other arises from the cubic Dresselhaus terms in combination with the cyclotron frequency.

The quantum dot is in the Coulomb blockade regime with extraction (U−U _ {-}U−​) and addition (U+U _ {+}U+​) energies that are large compared to the temperature, so that the dot occupation remains constant.
